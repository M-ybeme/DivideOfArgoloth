Aeloria

The Owlin's settlements in the forests are elegant and unobtrusive, constructed high in the trees and camouflaged against prying eyes. Their culture emphasizes adaptability and reflection, with a strong spiritual connection to their surroundings. Owlin leaders, known as *Nestwardens*, serve as both guides and philosophers, helping their people navigate the challenges of their new existence. The forests themselves are sacred to the Owlin, seen as both a refuge and a reminder of the Feywild's beauty. Rituals to honor the spirits of the woods are common, blending Feywild traditions with new practices developed in the Material Plane. The main settlement is called Aeloria by those that live there. Outsiders who speak of the "village in the sky" mostly refer to the group of buildings as the High Watch village. Although calling it a village is misleading.

Owlin History

Origins in the Feywild

The Owlin once thrived in the Feywild, a realm of boundless beauty and peril, where their silent feathers and keen senses made them exceptional hunters, scouts, and scholars. They lived in harmony with the plane's whimsical and chaotic nature, their societies flourishing in the twilight of eternal forests and moonlit glades. However, something changed. The Feywild, though enchanting, is a realm of extremes and unpredictability. Whether through political exile, a magical calamity, or a decision to seek stability, the Owlin were driven from their ancestral nests. The reasons for this exodus are veiled in secrecy, with Owlin elders speaking of it only in vague terms as “the Shattering.”

Arrival on the Material Plane

The Owlin's appearance on the Material Plane is a relatively recent event, occurring in the latter days of the last age. They came not in conquest but in search of a new home, their flocks scattered and their traditions strained by the demands of this alien realm. Guided by instinct and need, the Owlin settled in the ancient forests north of Marathport. These woods reminded them of their Feywild origins, offering both seclusion and safety. Here, the Owlin began to rebuild their culture. Though the Material Plane lacked the enchantments of the Feywild, it offered a grounding stability that allowed the Owlin to reflect, adapt, and grow.

The Connection to Marathport

The people of Marathport, who valued independence and philosophical inquiry, found kindred spirits in the Owlin. The two groups developed a quiet but profound connection based on shared values of contemplation, balance, and self-determination. While Marathport sought to preserve its sovereignty against the militaristic ambitions of Gatharion, the Owlin admired their resolve and communal spirit. Over time, this spiritual and philosophical closeness blossomed into practical cooperation. The Owlin, with their silent movement, exceptional perception, and natural aptitude for stealth, became invaluable to Marathport’s spy network. Their innate ability to blend into the shadows and move unseen made them perfect for intelligence gathering, sabotage, and counter-espionage.

Current Role and Legacy

The Owlin remain outsiders in many ways, their history as latecomers shaping their outlook on the world. They see themselves as observers and custodians, striving to preserve harmony in the face of conflict. Their loyalty to Marathport is deeply tied to their shared values, but they maintain a degree of independence, refusing to become tools of any one faction. Despite their late arrival, the Owlin have become a vital part of the region’s history and future. Their presence in the forests north of Marathport and their contributions to the city-state’s survival mark them as more than mere bystanders—they see themselves as the quiet architects of a new age.
