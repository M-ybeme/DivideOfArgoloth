Questions to Help Build a Full Character

Is your character from Anferdale?
  • Family and Role: Who are your family members, and what role do they play in Anferdale’s community? Are they farmers, fishers, traders, or artisans?
  • Traditions and Festivals: How does your character feel about Anferdale’s traditions, like the Festival of Nottyr?
  • Connections with Key Figures: Do you have relationships with any of the town’s key figures, like Harlan Ashhide or someone from the Greenfield or Deepwater families?
  • Mysteries and Legends: What’s one local legend or mystery your character is curious or skeptical about?
  • Favorite Spot in Town: Where does your character like to spend time in Anferdale?
  • Personal Loss or Rivalries: Has your character lost someone to the lake, the illness, or in the Water War of Anferdale?
  • Skills and Reputation: What skill or trait is your character known for in town?
  • Reason for Wanting to Leave: What drives your character to leave the town?
If they are not from Anferdale, where are they from?
  • Reason for Arrival: Why did you come to Anferdale?
  • First Impressions: What were your initial thoughts upon arriving?
  • Town Connections: Have you made any connections since arriving?
  • Outsider Status: How does your character feel about being an outsider in a close-knit community?
  • Seeking Something Specific: Is there a particular purpose driving you to Anferdale?
  • Personal Code or Beliefs: How does your character’s background clash with or complement Anferdale’s customs?
  • Skills That Stand Out: What skill or knowledge does your character possess that sets them apart?

Reasons for Classes in Remote Village

Click on a class to explore reasons why they might be found in a remote village setting:

  • Village Scout: Grew up exploring the surrounding forests, adept at sneaking, tracking, and gathering information.
  • Reformed Thief: Fell in with a bad crowd in youth but reformed, retaining skills in stealth and lockpicking.
  • Hunter/Trapper: Learned to move quietly and set traps as a hunter or trapper.

  • Village Guardian: Dedicated to protecting the village as a sacred duty.
  • Heirloom Armor: Inherited armor and a divine mission from a parent or grandparent.
  • Vision or Calling: Experienced a divine vision urging them to protect the innocent.

  • Village Militia: Trained to defend the village against bandits and wild animals.
  • Family Tradition: Comes from a family of warriors or guards, trained in combat since childhood.
  • Smith’s Apprentice: Learned combat and weapon skills as a blacksmith's apprentice.

  • Village Healer: Learned divine arts to help the sick and injured.
  • Religious Devotion: Deeply religious and involved in rituals and ceremonies.
  • Miracle Event: A miraculous event or intervention led them to their faith.

  • Nature Protector: Growing up in a village surrounded by nature, they developed a deep bond with the land and its creatures.
  • Herbalist: Working as the village herbalist, they became attuned to the natural world and its magical properties.
  • Wilderness Guide: They often guided travelers and hunters through the nearby forests, learning the ways of nature and developing druidic powers.

  • Hunter and Tracker: As the village’s primary hunter, they became skilled in tracking, survival, and archery, making them a natural ranger.
  • Forest Guardian: They took it upon themselves to protect the village from threats emerging from the surrounding wilderness, developing their ranger abilities in the process.
  • Lone Explorer: They have always been a bit of a loner, exploring the wild areas around the village, developing a deep understanding of the natural world and its dangers.

  • Village Entertainer: Known for their storytelling, music, and performances at village festivals and gatherings, they naturally developed into a bard.
  • Lore Keeper: They took on the role of preserving the village’s history and legends, using their skills to entertain and educate.
  • Traveling Performer: Before settling in the village, they might have traveled with a troupe of performers, learning their craft before deciding to stay.

  • Innate Magic: They discovered their magical abilities by accident, perhaps due to a magical ancestor. Their powers manifested naturally, and they’ve learned to control them over time.
  • Local Prodigy: Recognized early on for their innate magical talent, they were mentored by a visiting mage or a knowledgeable village elder.
  • Wild Magic Event: A significant event, like a storm or magical phenomenon, triggered their latent powers, leading them to explore and develop their sorcerous abilities.

  • Scholar of the Village: They were always curious and studious, spending time learning from any books or scrolls they could find. A visiting wizard might have given them their first spellbook.
  • Apprentice: They apprenticed under a reclusive wizard living nearby or an elder who had some arcane knowledge.
  • Artifact Discovery: They found a magical artifact or tome that unlocked their potential and interest in wizardry, leading them to study magic more deeply.

  • Temple Training: They trained at a small monastery or temple near the village, focusing on martial arts, meditation, and spiritual discipline.
  • Self-Discipline: Known for their discipline and self-control, they practiced martial arts as a form of meditation and defense.
  • Wandering Monk: A wandering monk passed through the village and took them as an apprentice, teaching them the ways of the monk.

  • Pact Discovery: They stumbled upon an ancient relic or book that connected them to a powerful patron, granting them magical abilities.
  • Desperation: In a moment of desperation or need, they made a pact with a powerful being for the power to protect their village or achieve a personal goal.
  • Mystical Encounter: They had a mystical encounter with a supernatural being who offered them power, and they accepted the pact out of curiosity or necessity.

  • Outsider: They come from a distant land or tribe, seeking refuge or a new life in the village.
  • Protector: They were chosen by their tribe or village to be a protector, using their rage and strength to defend their people.
  • Survivor: They survived a traumatic event that awakened their primal instincts and rage, leading them to embrace their barbarian nature.
